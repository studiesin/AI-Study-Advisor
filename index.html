2. Target Destinations: Public vs. Private

The desired institution radically alters the admission strategy. Public universities are heavily subsidized but highly competitive, relying strictly on an objective numeric score (Nota de Corte). Private universities are more costly but offer fewer bureaucratic hurdles.

🏫

Public Universities

Strict admission via up to 14 points. High competition requires extra PCE exams for top degrees. Low tuition fees.

🏢

Private Universities

Admission via internal testing and interviews. Lower bureaucratic barriers, but significantly higher tuition costs.

4. The Unique 14-Point Grading Scale

A standard homologated foreign high school GPA is converted by UNEDasiss to a scale that strictly caps at 10.0 points. However, top public universities demand cut-off marks reaching up to 14.0.

The crucial extra 4 points are exclusively earned through Pruebas de Competencias Específicas (PCE) exams.

The +4 Calculation Formula:

Final Grade = Base (Max 10) + (PCE 1 × 0.2) + (PCE 2 × 0.2)

Each specific PCE subject is scored out of 10. A passing grade (greater than 5) is multiplied by a university-assigned weighting factor (0.1 or 0.2, maximum +2 points each) and added directly to the base score.
